Study of Rivers as Complex Networks

Guide: Mr. Vibhuti Bhushan Jha (Scientist/Engineer‚ÄìSD, ISRO) | Institution: Dharmsinh Desai University

Explored the topological and spectral characteristics of river networks using complex network theory at ISRO's Space Applications Centre. Analyzed river basins as interconnected systems to identify structural patterns relevant to hydrology, geomorphology, and environmental modeling.

Research Objectives

  • Model river drainage systems as complex networks using graph theory
  • Extract and process geospatial data (Digital Elevation Models and shapefiles) to represent real river basins
  • Analyze connectivity, scaling laws, and spectral features of river systems to understand universal properties
  • Compare real-world river networks with theoretical models such as Optimal Channel Networks (OCN)

Methodology & Key Findings

  • Extracted river basins and networks from HydroSHEDS DEM data using QGIS and Python-based tools
  • Converted river shapefiles into graph representations (nodes and edges) and computed adjacency matrices
  • Analyzed eigenvalue spectra, betweenness centrality, closeness centrality, degree distributions, and spectral gap
  • Validated that river networks exhibited finite-width eigenvalue distributions with measurable spectral gaps, independent of basin size and shape
  • Confirmed consistency with Optimal Channel Network (OCN) models, demonstrating universality in river network topology
  • Demonstrated application of spectral graph theory to hydrological and ecological systems for connectivity and stability inference

Impact

The study validated the applicability of complex network theory to real-world river systems and suggested a potential framework for large-scale hydrological modeling and environmental data interpretation. Future extensions include cross-planetary analysis of drainage networks (e.g., Mars, Moon) using spectral topology methods.
