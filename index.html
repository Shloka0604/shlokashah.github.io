ISRO Research Internship

Study of Rivers as Complex Networks

Space Applications Centre, ISRO | December 2022 - March 2023
Guide: Mr. Vibhuti Bhushan Jha (Scientist/Engineer-SD)

Research Focus

Explored topological and spectral characteristics of river networks using complex network theory to analyze river basins as interconnected systems and identify structural patterns relevant to hydrology, geomorphology, and environmental modeling.

Methodology

1
Data Extraction
River basins and networks from HydroSHEDS DEM data using QGIS and Python
2
Graph Representation
Converted river shapefiles into graph representations (nodes and edges)
3
Network Analysis
Computed adjacency matrices and analyzed eigenvalue spectra, betweenness centrality, closeness centrality, degree distributions
Python NetworkX QGIS NumPy Matplotlib Spectral Graph Theory

Key Findings

  • River networks exhibited finite-width eigenvalue distributions with measurable spectral gaps, independent of basin size and shape
  • Spectral features consistent with Optimal Channel Network (OCN) models, confirming universality in river network topology
  • Validated applicability of complex network theory to real-world hydrological systems
  • Suggested framework for large-scale environmental modeling and cross-planetary drainage analysis

Smart Car Parking System

Verilog & Quartus FPGA Simulation

Developed a secure, automated parking management system using Finite State Machines (FSM) implemented in Verilog and simulated in Intel Quartus II. The project aimed to reduce human involvement and optimize parking slot utilization through logic-based automation.

Verilog Intel Quartus II FSM Design Digital Logic
1
FSM Design
Moore-type FSM to control gate access, LED indicators, and state transitions across five conditions
2
Security Implementation
Password-based authentication for vehicle entry and exit
3
Sensor Integration
Entrance and exit sensors to track vehicle flow and update slot availability
4
Verification
Logic verified through waveform simulation in Quartus

Impact & Extensions

Demonstrated a scalable, cost-effective parking solution with real-time monitoring and minimal hardware usage. Proposed extensions include multi-slot parking with LCD-based vacancy display and unique access keys for enhanced safety.
